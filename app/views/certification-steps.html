How Certification Works

There are 9 steps to go through before you can be certified to join the IAX.

Step 1 - start certification

Complete the application form to start the certification process. Return it to the governing body at iax@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk

Once you’ve been certified, you’ll be a member of the IAX.

The form contains details about:

  • your organisation
  • your service
  • how you meet the IAX’s standards and rules

Your form will be reviewed by the governing body to ensure you meet the IAX’s standards and rules.

The governing body will confirm if you’re ready to work with an auditor and begin the certification process.

Step 2 - start to work with an auditor

Check the IAX’s approved auditor list and find an auditor you’d like to work with.

Once you’ve been certified, you’ll be a member of the IAX.

You should agree how:

  • your relationship will work, for example in person or on email
  • much you’ll pay your auditor
  • long the certification process is likely to take

Your auditor will answer any questions you have at this stage, and throughout the certification process.

Step 3 - pre-assessment checks with your auditor

The governing body will send your chosen auditor your application form.

Your auditor will review it and check that you meet the IAX’s:

  • standards and rules
  • pre-assessment criteria

They’ll ask for any evidence they need to see at this stage, for example proof of company registration.

Step 4 - start the assessment process

You’ll need to:

  • provide a description of your service
  • complete the base criteria and any other criteria relevant to you

Your auditor will give you the necessary documents to complete and also guide you about the:

  • level of detail you need to provide
  • specific areas to focus on

Step 5 - review the evidence

If necessary, your auditor will contact you to:

  • discuss the evidence you provided
  • ask for further evidence
  • answer any questions you have
  • discuss what happens next

Depending on your role in the IAX, this may be the end of the process or your auditor may recommend an on-site audit.

Step 6 - the on-site audit

The on-site audit will usually take about 2 days.

Your auditor will tell you:

  • possible dates for the audit
  • what they need to see during the on-site audit
  • who they’ll need to see
  • what areas they’ll be testing, for example security

You should find a date that suits you both and when you can be certain that the auditor will see everybody and everything they need to.

Your auditor will send you an agenda so you know what to expect.

Step 7 - the on-site audit

The on-site audit will usually take about 2 days.

Your auditor will tell you:

  • possible dates for the audit
  • what they need to see during the on-site audit
  • who they’ll need to see
  • what areas they’ll be testing, for example security

You should find a date that suits you both and when you can be certain that the auditor will see everybody and everything they need to.

Your auditor will send you an agenda so you know what to expect.

Step 8 - after the on-site audit

At the end of the on-site audit, your auditor will let you know how it went.

Your auditor may say:

  • this is the end of your assessment
  • you need to make some changes before they can complete their assessment

Your auditor will send you an audit report which lists:

  • when and how the audit was performed
  • the participants
  • what was assessed
  • any fixes you may need to make before you can be certified

Step 9 - recommendation to the governing body

Your auditor will send their audit report to the governing body along with the results of your assessment.

Step 10 - join the IAX

The governing body will review the auditor’s decision and contact you within 2 weeks to confirm you can join the IAX.
