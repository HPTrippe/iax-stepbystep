What you get from being part of the Identity and Attributes Exchange

Save time, effort and money

You'll get a trust mark when you become a trusted provider in the IAX scheme. This proves you follow government and industry guidance on how to:

  • stop criminals or users that are pretending to be someone else
  • run a secure product or service

The trust mark will also prove your product or service can be used safely.

Show other schemes you meet their standards

Organisations that are part of schemes in other industries (such as finance, health and retail) might be certified against equivalent standards.

Being certified to join the IAX could reduce the time and effort you spend on getting certified to join other schemes. In some cases it can even mean you will not need to go through another full certification process.

Preparing to be certified takes time and can cost some organisations up to £45,000 each time.

Security built in

Being part of the IAX means everybody will have to meet the same technical specifications.

Being part of the IAX means everybody will have to meet a level of security controls appropriate to the technology they use.

That means you can trust that everyone involved is meeting the same levels of security, which could mean fewer system breaches.

Building trust

If you’re part of the trust framework, the rest of the public sector and the private sector know what standards you meet and that you can be trusted.

This creates a chain of trust and puts the public sector at the forefront of creating the standards for digital identity.

Make it easier for users to do things online

You'll know exactly how other organisations on the IAX scheme have created their digital identities or attributes because you’ll meet the same standards. You can trust that any information you get from other participants is accurate and reliable.

Sharing identities and attributes will ultimately make it easier for users to do things online. This is because they'll no longer need to prove their identity and eligibility every time they interact with the government.

Sell products and services to the UK government

The IAX is currently the only digital identity scheme that lets you sell your products and services to the UK government.

Become a trusted provider

There are several roles in the IAX. You can be:

  • an identity provider
  • an attribute provider
  • a broker

Find out more about the different roles in the scheme.

You must be certified by an independent auditor before you can join the scheme. The auditor will make sure the products or services you supply meet the IAX's standards.
